## 📊 Project Overview

This Annual Live Births Dashboard provides comprehensive insights into birth patterns across Malaysia over 24 years (2000-2023). The dashboard helps understand demographic trends, regional variations, ethnic distributions, and gender ratios in live births.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- **Microsoft Excel**
  - Pivot Tables for data aggregation
  - Pivot Charts for visualization
  - Slicers for interactive filtering
  - Conditional Formatting for highlighting trends
  - Formulas for calculations (SUMIFS, COUNTIFS, AVERAGE, etc.)
 
---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Track annual live birth trends over 24 years
- Analyze state-wise distribution of births
- Compare male vs female birth ratios
- Understand ethnic composition of births
- Identify regional patterns across Malaysia
- Provide insights for healthcare planning and policy

---

### Key Time Period
- **Date Range:** 2000 - 2023 (24 years)
- **Geography:** Malaysia (States and Regions)
- **Demographics:** Ethnicity, Gender

### 7. Key Demographic Insights

**Population Implications**

1. **Peak Fertility Period:** 2014 marked the highest birth year (202,619), indicating a demographic peak

2. **Pandemic Impact:** Sharp decline 2020-2022 (-16.5% from 2019 to 2022)

3. **Recovery Sign:** 2023 shows strong rebound (+8.12%)

4. **Gender Balance:** Consistent natural ratio of ~107 males per 100 females

5. **Ethnic Composition:** Bumi population (74.31%) dominates births

**Regional Population Shifts:**
- **Southern Region** (37.99%) is the demographic center
- **Federal Territories** low birth share (5.34%) reflects urban costs
- **East Malaysia** (18.76%) significant contribution

---

### 8. Strategic Recommendations

**For Healthcare Planners**

1. **Resource Allocation by Birth Volume:**
   - **Priority 1:** Southern Region (4.48M births) - 38% of all births
   - **Priority 2:** Northern Region (2.36M births) - 20% of births
   - **Priority 3:** East Malaysia (2.21M births) - 19% of births

2. **Maternity Services:**
   - Selangor needs 2.3x capacity of average state
   - Johor, Sabah, Sarawak require major maternity centers

3. **Neonatal Care:**
   - Plan NICUs based on birth volumes by region
   - Distribute midwives proportionally to births

**For Education Planners**

1. **Future School Enrollment:**
   - 2014 peak birth cohort entering universities now
   - 2022 low birth cohort will impact primary schools in 2028-2029
   - Plan for 8% enrollment increase from 2023 rebound

2. **Regional School Planning:**
   - Southern Region needs 38% of new school capacity
   - Federal Territories need only 5% of new capacity

**For Economic Planners**

1. **Workforce Projection:**
   - 2014 peak cohort entering workforce 2032-2036
   - 2022 low cohort will create workforce gap 2040-2045

2. **Housing and Infrastructure:**
   - Focus family housing in Southern and Northern Regions
   - Plan pediatric healthcare in high-birth areas
